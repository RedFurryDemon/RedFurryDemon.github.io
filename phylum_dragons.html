[DIVISIO] Dracomorpha - draconids

Draconids are the most advanced taxon within the reptile phylogenetic tree. They are found in almost all environments and greatly vary in size, diet, and social behavior. Earliest forms were quadrupedal, terrestrial omnivores covered in spikes and hardened scales.

[SUBORDO] Serpentiformes - wyrms, or "great serpents"

Despite their outward appearance, wyrms are not related to snakes any more closely than wyverns and drakes. They have movable eyelids, mandibles that are joined at the front of the mouth, no vomeronasal organs, and other anatomical characteristics typical to draconids. A single pair of horns or a spinous crest may be present. Wyrms are exclusively carnivorous, with most being ambush predators. Many species are aquatic. Arboreal species have prehensile tail used also to help launching themselves at prey.

[SUBORDO] Vivernae - wyverns

Small-to-medium flying draconids.Their front limbs have four fingers, of which three are greatly elongated and support the wing membrane. Spinous crest is likewise membranous and acts as a rudder during flight. All wyverns are ectotherms; their wings are also used for thermoregulation.
